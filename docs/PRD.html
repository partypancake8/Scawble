How to use this document

This is written to be executed by an autonomous coding loop, not just read.

Every section below is a contract. The design tokens, interaction timings, data models, and definition of done are literal — copy them, don't reinterpret them. The build loop at the end is the execution order, and each phase has an exit gate: an objective, testable condition that must pass before moving on. An agent runs the loop top to bottom, self-verifying at each gate.

Honest caveat. A single autonomous pass producing a fully polished, ship-quality game is at the frontier of what's reliable today — "feel" in particular is judged, not compiled. This spec is built to maximize a clean run by making every subjective thing objective (exact curves, durations, colors, acceptance tests) and by structuring the work behind self-checking gates. Expect the loop to iterate on the feel phase against its checklist rather than nail it in one shot. The spec is the thing that makes that iteration convergent instead of endless.

01

North star — NYT Crossplay

Primary reference: NYT Crossplay (launched Jan 2026), NYT Games' Scrabble-like tile game — we replicate its solo-vs-bot slice and its rules. Secondary reference: the broader NYT Games craft bar (Wordle/Connections/Crossword) — the typography, daily cadence, stats/streak screens, and restraint that make the whole app feel considered. Build to both: Crossplay for what the game is, NYT-at-large for how it should feel.

◆ We replicate from Crossplay

  • Rebalanced tiles — N/R/S/T = 1pt, K/V/W/Y = 5–6pt; rewards clever placement over hoarding high-value letters (§03)
  • Fair endgame — when the bag empties, both sides get an equal number of final turns (their "last turn" rule)
  • CrossBot-style analysis — every move reviewed, best play surfaced, rated on luck and strategy (§05) — our marquee, and it's inherently single-player
  • Clean, board-focused UI — "no cartoon graphics, no interruptions"; the opposite of Words With Friends
  • Solo-vs-bot, no time pressure — Crossplay ships this exact mode; it's our whole v1
  • NYT restraint — typographic confidence, generous whitespace, brief earned celebration, instant offline no-login core, real accessibility

◇ We deliberately avoid

  • Cartoon/casino aesthetics, coin showers, mid-game ad interruptions
  • Login walls before first play
  • Cluttered HUDs; competing accent colors
  • Long unskippable celebration sequences
  • Energy timers / dark-pattern monetization

What we drop (for v1). Crossplay's core is live two-player (invite friends / skill-matched strangers). We cut all of that and lean into the parts that stand alone: the solo bot game, the daily, and the CrossBot-style analysis. Adding async play-a-friend later is the natural next step.

Tile values & bag — Crossplay-style rebalance (our own IP)

Values rebalanced toward Crossplay's philosophy (common consonants cheap, K/V/W/Y expensive) — rewards placement over hoarding, and gives us our own distribution rather than Scrabble's trademarked one. Counts stay at the standard 100-tile frequencies (values changed, not counts). Tune in Phase 1.

Rules the feel must respect

  • All animation runs on the UI thread (Reanimated worklets / Skia) — never drop below 60fps during drag.
  • Every effect increases legibility of game state. If an effect hides what happened, cut it.
  • prefers-reduced-motion ⇒ replace all transforms with 1-frame cross-fades; keep haptics + sound.
  • Sound + haptics are independently toggleable; pitch is randomized so repeats never feel canned.
  • Celebration is brief and skippable — a tap dismisses instantly (NYT restraint).

Feel acceptance test

Record a 10-second screen capture of: pick up a tile → drag → snap → submit a bingo. It passes when (1) drag never stutters, (2) the snap reads as physical (squash present), (3) haptic fires within 16ms of visual snap, (4) the bingo celebration is under 700ms and dismissable. If any fail, iterate — do not proceed to screen assembly.

05

Screens — behavior & acceptance

Five screens ship in v1. Each is specified by layout, behavior, and a pass condition.

Screen · Home

Daily hub

Layout: wordmark, today's date, a single primary "Play today's puzzle" card, secondary tiles for Classic (vs bot) and Solo puzzle, current streak in the corner.

Behavior: resumes an in-progress game if one exists; otherwise starts fresh. No login. Deep-linkable to the daily.

Pass Cold-open to interactive Home in under 1s; streak reflects local history.
Screen · Board

The game

Layout: 15×15 board (fits width, premium squares subtly colored per tokens), rack of 7 below, controls: Shuffle, Recall, Swap, Submit, Pass. Score header: you vs. bot, tiles-in-bag count.

Behavior: full §04 interaction spec. Real-time move validity (Submit disabled until a legal placement exists). Bot plays with a visible "thinking" beat (≥400ms even if instant) then animates its word in.

Pass A complete game can be played start→finish with correct scoring and no illegal move accepted.
Screen · Analysis — the marquee

ScawBot review (à la CrossBot)

Layout: a full-game timeline of your moves; per turn shows your play, the best available play (mini-board overlay + score), and the delta. Two headline ratings for the game: Strategy (how close your plays were to optimal) and Luck (tile draw quality vs. average).

Behavior: replays the recorded GameState.history, re-running bestMove at each position. Strategy = mean(yourScore / bestScore); Luck = your draw's expected value vs. the distribution mean. Surfaces your single best word and your biggest miss. This is inherently single-player and is the feature that makes v1 worth opening without an opponent.

Pass Best play per turn is provably the top-scoring legal move; Strategy/Luck numbers reconcile with the stored history on replay.
Screen · Stats

Streaks & history

Layout: NYT-style: current streak, max streak, games played, win %, best word, a distribution/graph. Big serif numbers.

Behavior: derived entirely from local persisted results; shareable summary (text, no image needed for v1).

Pass Numbers reconcile exactly with stored game history.
Screen · Settings

Controls

Layout: sound, haptics, reduced-motion override, theme (auto/light/dark), difficulty default, cosmetics (tile skin scaffold).

Pass Every toggle persists and takes effect immediately.

07

Data models & core contracts

These interfaces are the API surface between modules. Implement to them exactly.

type Letter = 'A'..'Z'; type Blank = '_';
interface Tile   { id: string; letter: Letter | Blank; value: number; assigned?: Letter } // blank's chosen letter
interface Cell   { row: number; col: number; premium?: 'DL'|'TL'|'DW'|'TW'|'★'; tile?: Tile }
interface Placement { tile: Tile; row: number; col: number }
interface Move   { placements: Placement[]; words: string[]; score: number; isBingo: boolean }

interface GameState {
  board: Cell[15][15]; bag: Tile[]; racks: { player: Tile[]; bot: Tile[] };
  scores: { player: number; bot: number }; turn: 'player'|'bot';
  history: Move[]; seed: string; over: boolean;
}

// engine
legalPlacements(state, rack): Placement[][]
scoreMove(board, placements): { score, words, isBingo }
applyMove(state, move): GameState        // pure, returns next state

// lexicon (swappable)
interface Lexicon { isWord(w: string): boolean; size: number; name: string }

// ai
generateMoves(state, rack): Move[]                    // GADDAG, all legal, ranked by score
chooseMove(moves, difficulty): Move                   // tier picks from ranked list
bestMove(state, rack): Move                           // for Analysis screen

// daily determinism
seedForDate(date): string                             // same board+draw for everyone that day

Determinism. Bag shuffle and blank-selection are driven by seedForDate so the Daily is identical for every player and replayable. Classic mode uses a random seed.

08

AI opponent — spec

Generator
GADDAG built from the active lexicon (Gordon 1994). Enumerates every legal move for a rack+board in <50ms.
Evaluation
score + leaveValue(remainingRack). Leave values reward retaining letters likely to form bingos (borrow Quackle's table approach).
Tiers
Casual: pick from top 40–70% band + noise. Skilled: top ~15%. Expert: best static eval. Brutal: best eval + 2-ply simulation (~200 rollouts).
Timing
Always show ≥400ms "thinking" beat so instant moves still feel deliberate; hard-cap 1.5s.
Determinism
Bot RNG seeded from game seed + turn index for reproducible Dailies.

Refs: Gordon 1994 (GADDAG, PDF) · Quackle (BSD) · How Quackle plays

Content & modes

Daily
  • One puzzle/day, seeded by date — identical board + tile draw for everyone.
  • Goal: beat the bot (Expert) and/or beat the "par" score. Feeds the streak.
Classic
  • Full game vs. bot at chosen difficulty, random seed, replayable freely.
Solo puzzle
  • "Find the best word" — given a fixed rack + board, score as high as you can in one move. A bite-sized brain-teaser using bestMove as the target.

Definition of Done

v1 ships only when every box is objectively true. This is the loop's global success condition.

  • Engine — unit tests cover scoring (incl. cross-words, premium stacking, bingo, blanks), connectivity, first-move-on-center; all pass.
  • Lexicon — isWord matches the ENABLE list on a 1000-word sample; unknown words rejected.
  • AI — bestMove proven optimal on ≥20 hand-checked positions; four tiers produce distinguishable strength over 100 sim games.
  • Feel — the §04 acceptance test passes on device; 60fps held during drag; reduced-motion path verified.
  • Screens — each of the five passes its stated condition; full game playable start→finish.
  • Daily — same date yields identical puzzle across two fresh installs.
  • Persistence — stats/streak survive app restart and reconcile with history.
  • A11y — contrast ≥ WCAG AA, hit targets ≥ 44px, VoiceOver labels on controls.
  • Build — runs on iOS device via TestFlight with no red-screen errors.
